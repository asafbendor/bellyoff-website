## The Science Behind Shorter, Smarter Workouts

### How Your Body Changes After 40

After 40, your body handles prolonged exercise differently than it did at 25. Muscle recovery takes longer. Joints need more care. Your nervous system fatigues faster from sustained exertion. These aren't weaknesses; they're signals that your body prefers quality over quantity.

Your metabolism also shifts. The window for fat loss during exercise narrows. This means a 60-minute session doesn't give you six times the benefit of a 10-minute one. Instead, the extra 50 minutes often leads to:

- Elevated cortisol (stress hormone) that promotes belly fat storage
- Muscle breakdown instead of preservation
- Joint strain and delayed recovery
- Mental burnout that kills long-term consistency


### The Cortisol Problem with Long Workouts

Extended exercise sessions trigger cortisol release, especially when intensity stays moderate. For adults over 40, this is counterproductive. Chronically elevated cortisol directly increases visceral belly fat - the dangerous kind linked to heart disease and metabolic issues.

A 10-minute, intentionally designed workout keeps cortisol in the optimal zone. You get the fat-burning benefits without the metabolic backlash.
